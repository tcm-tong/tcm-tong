About our Firm :

The teaching of the 5 elements or 5 phases of change is a central part of the Chinese view of the world. Each individual can be assigned to one of these elements (water, fire, metal, wood and earth); there is a defined relationship of forces and effects between the elements. According to Traditional Chinese Medicine, the 5 elements and their 2 basic cycles (the cycle of mutual nourishing and transforming and the overcoming cycle) are an expression of the fact that all organs and all possible ailments are interconnected within a functional circle. The 5 phases of change (spring, summer, interim, autumn and winter) describe the different qualities and states inherent in natural phenomena. Derived from the observation of nature, these theories serve to describe the interactions between humans and the environment, as well as between the individual organs within the human organism.

Our Philosophy:

At the heart of Traditional Chinese Medicine is the belief that opposites complement each other and are mutually dependent (e.g. strong and weak, cold and warm, male and female). Traditional Chinese Medicine divides all the energy and organs of the body into yin and yang. Yin and Yang are both opposite and complementary principles, respectively forces. These forces describe states that are not rigid or absolute, but are always changing. Every yin contains a portion of yang - and vice versa. Events or structures that are directed inwards, that absorb and gather strength, show the principle of yin. The yang, on the other hand, goes outwards and upwards, tending to become exhausted. When yin and yang are in harmony, the qi (life energy) flows freely.
